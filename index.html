March 18 - 21, 2025

During the UIC College of Computer Science Cebu Educational Tour 2025, which takes place from March 18 - 21, our focus is to expose students to real-world applications of computer science principles. This includes a special visit to JCI Cebu, one of the premier technology hubs in the region. This tour is designed to provide valuable industry insights and networking opportunities with leading IT professionals.

The day began with a warm welcome from the Alliance Company's CEO, who introduced us to their cutting-edge facilities. The students were given a comprehensive overview of the company’s operations, which specialize in software development and tech solutions. This session was particularly insightful as it aligned with our curriculum's emphasis on practical applications of computer science.

Tour Experiences

Day 1: Our educational excursion commenced with an exploration of Cebu City's renowned attractions. We initiated our journey at SM Seaside Cebu, one of the country's expansive malls, providing us an opportunity to unwind and navigate through its contemporary amenities. Subsequently, we ventured to Fort San Pedro, the oldest triangular bastion fort in the Philippines, gaining insights into its Spanish colonial past. Our visit then continued to the Minor Basilica of the Holy Child of Cebu (Basilica Minore del Santo Niño), a pivotal church steeped in profound religious and cultural importance. We concluded our day at the National Museum in Cebu, where we admired a diverse collection of artifacts, artworks, and historic exhibits that eloquently narrated Cebu’s rich cultural legacy. This day primarily focused on cultural immersion and fostering camaraderie among our peers before we transitioned to the more technical aspects of our tour.

Day 2: The second day of our tour was dedicated to delving into the professional aspects of our field. We began at Alliance Software Inc., an IT solutions company based in Cebu, where we learned about their organizational culture, development practices, and career prospects. This visit provided us with a firsthand look at the operations of a successful local software company. In the afternoon, we participated in a seminar hosted by JCI Cebu, featuring a lineup of guest speakers from several local software and technology companies. This session stood out as a tour highlight, offering insights from: Jade Lobrigas – Technical Manager at InspireX - Philippines Marvin Bonifacio – Lead Tech Architecture Associate Director at Accenture Technology Cebu Lance Pacio – Head of Products at Xeleqt Technology Innovations Inc. Mitchell Edward Howell – Owner of Frozen Lake Software Charlo Nocete – Concept Artist at Pixel Mafia Allen Sampaga – 3D Artist at Pixel Mafia Lauren Lim – 2D Artist at Pixel Mafia Avin Ebeza Baclay – PR and Marketing at APEX PR and Marketing Each presenter shared their personal stories, company details, and professional insights, enriching our understanding of the local technology landscape and potential career opportunities.

Day 3: On the third day, we visited GoTeam, a company known for its remote staffing services and strong emphasis on company culture and innovation. We got an inside look at their work environment, tools, and how they manage distributed teams, which was especially relevant in today’s remote-first world. After the company visit, we spent the rest of the day visiting famous Cebu landmarks. We explored the peaceful Taoist Temple, admired the majestic Temple of Leah, and ended the evening with a memorable dinner at Tops Cebu, a popular spot known for its breathtaking panoramic view of the city skyline.

Day 4: On our final day, we had the opportunity to explore the operations of Mactan Cebu International Airport through a guided facility tour. It was interesting to see how technology plays a vital role in modern airport logistics, from passenger processing to security systems. After the tour, we concluded our educational journey and headed back home, bringing with us not only souvenirs but also knowledge, inspiration, and unforgettable memories.
